Titanic is one of the highest grossing and most iconic Hollywood movies of the 20th century. The story of Jack and Rose is engrained in the memory of millions as the modern love story. However, people will probably be surprised as Jack and Rose are not even real. As we idolized over the movie, we don’t really understand the facts and figure behind the sinking of the S.S.Titanic, one of the most tragic and influential sea disasters of the early 20th century. The legacy of this accident lives on today as modern ships and cruises are all equipped with better technology and procedures.

Through this interactive experience, we want to tell the story and pay our respects to those who perish in this tragic accident with the hope that their story will not only be a constant reminder of the need of proper safety procedure, but also the need for a more inclusive and fair society. As you scroll through the interactive graphs, we will layout facts and statistics of the accident which includes deep dive of the passenger and crew’s nationality, age, gender, social class and how these factors affect their survival.

The majority were aged between 15 and 45 years old.
This graph shows the distribution of the and age group distribution. Titanic was a passenger liner that operated between England and the United States. It carried some of the wealthiest people in Europe, but also at the same time carried immigrants into the US. It is thus no surprise that working age individuals (age 15 - 30) account for the majority of the passenger as the early 20th century was a time where European migration into the US was at a high due to wars and instability in Europe. There was a much larger proportion of passengers in 3rd class.
This graph shows the distribution of passenger class. Even though Titanic was infamous as one of the most luxurious ships back at its day, it actually made a lot of its earnings carrying poor immigrants from Europe to the US. The rooms and accomodation that these people had (3rd class passengers) were much less luxurious and often lacking. Data shows that there is a much larger proportion of 3rd class passengers compared to other classes, but 1st and 2nd class passenger numbers were similar.
Where are the passengers from?
In the 21st century, we are used to thinking that globalization and movement of people is a recent trend. However, it was because of early pioneer innovation such as Titanic that people can move almost anywhere they want and within reasonable time. We see that the Titanic had a very diverse set of nationalities. It not only had passengers from Europe, but also from far away places such as Asia and the middle east.
